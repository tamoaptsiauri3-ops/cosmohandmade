# OpenAI Credit Use Plan

This document explains how COSMO Open Workshop Digital Toolkit would use OpenAI API credits in a practical, measurable and open-source way.

## 1. Structured product catalogue workflows

Credits will be used to prototype workflows that convert unstructured product information into clean product records.

Inputs may include:

- product name;
- material notes;
- dimensions;
- color options;
- personalization options;
- care instructions;
- production notes;
- wholesale notes;
- circularity and offcut information.

Expected output:

- structured JSON records;
- WooCommerce-ready product fields;
- marketplace-ready product descriptions;
- B2B line-sheet fields.

## 2. Georgian-English content generation

Credits will support prompt and evaluation development for product storytelling in Georgian and English.

The goal is not to create generic luxury language. The goal is to create clear, warm, export-ready copy that explains craft, function, materials, personalization and story.

Expected output:

- Georgian product descriptions;
- English product descriptions;
- SEO titles and meta descriptions;
- care and material notes;
- short B2B story blocks.

## 3. Personalization and gift-builder assistant

Credits will help prototype an assistant that guides customers or sales teams through personalized gift choices.

The assistant should collect:

- product type;
- color preference;
- budget range;
- initials or engraving text;
- gift occasion;
- packaging preference;
- quantity;
- deadline;
- corporate branding needs.

Expected output:

- customer-facing recommendation;
- production-ready personalization brief;
- missing-information checklist.

## 4. B2B catalogue and buyer readiness

Credits will help generate buyer-facing documentation from structured data.

Expected output:

- product line sheets;
- MOQ and delivery-time notes;
- export-ready product summaries;
- concept store and museum-shop positioning blocks;
- concise wholesale outreach drafts.

## 5. Circular waste-to-product documentation

Credits will support documentation of leather and textile offcut reuse.

The workflow will help teams describe how production waste is sorted, reused and transformed into functional design objects such as small accessories, interior pieces or furniture prototypes.

Expected output:

- offcut tracking notes;
- before/after documentation;
- material-use summaries;
- circular design story blocks;
- non-greenwashing review checklist.

## 6. Evaluation and reliability

Credits will also be used to build test cases and evaluation examples so the toolkit is safe for non-technical teams.

Evaluation will check whether outputs are:

- factually grounded in provided product data;
- clear in Georgian and English;
- not exaggerated;
- not falsely claiming sustainability impact;
- useful for e-commerce, B2B and workshop contexts;
- complete enough for sales and production teams.

## Public outputs

The repository will publish reusable schemas, prompts, examples and documentation so other small creative businesses can adapt the toolkit.
